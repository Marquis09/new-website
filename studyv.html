Project Coward: Interactive ARG Interface

From Wholesome Community Portal to Glitch-Driven Psychological Horror

Background

Project Coward began as a collaborative Alternate Reality Game (ARG) experiment exploring digital personas, cyberbullying, and psychological horror through the lens of a fictional Vtuber influencer. The central hub (the VTuber Nexus) served as the entry point for players, beginning as a wholesome, inviting space before gradually degrading into a corrupted, glitch-filled environment that mirrored the character’s emotional deterioration.

My role centered on designing and engineering this dual-state interface, ensuring that every visual shift, glitch effect, and narrative beat aligned with the psychological arc of the character. The website needed to feel alive, responsive to player actions, connected to external platforms, and capable of evolving in real time.

Process Index: 1. The Vision | 2. Interactive Engineering | 3. Evolution | 4. Prototype | 5. Testing | 6. Technical Challenges | 7. Reflection

Executive Summary

  • Challenge: Build an immersive web terminal for an ARG that bridges web infrastructure with live external platforms.
  • Solution: A dual-state interface using dynamic DOM mutation, secure portal verification, and cross-platform narrative integration.

1. The Vision: Immersive Environmental Storytelling

The VTuber Nexus was designed to feel like a living environment, a space that evolved alongside the narrative. The wholesome aesthetic of the early interface represented Stella’s innocence and approachability, while the later corrupted state reflected her psychological unraveling under cyberbullying pressure.

Moodboard (Inspiration)

This transformation wasn’t just visual; it was emotional. Every glitch, distortion, and UI breakdown was engineered to reinforce the narrative themes of identity erosion and digital vulnerability.

To ensure our code paradigms and design assets moved smoothly across external platform environments, I worked heavily across disciplines: bridging design and code with our 2D Lead Artist, managing cross-platform infrastructure with Writing and PR leads, and synthesizing multimedia fragments into a responsive grid capable of handling variable cross-origin referrals.

2. Interactive Engineering: The Dual-State Architecture

The interface was built around a dual-state architecture: a stable, wholesome environment and a corrupted, horror-driven counterpart. This required careful engineering to ensure seamless transitions triggered by user interactions.

  • The Portal Gate: A secure login system using hardcoded credentials (admin / password123) discovered through narrative breadcrumbs.
  • The Record Trigger: A UI interaction that initiates glitch effects, marking the transition into the horror phase.
  • Dynamic Theme Degradation: DOM overrides, corrupted image swaps, and altered merchandise pricing simulate psychological collapse.

4. Responsive Testing Grounds: Live Prototype & Mechanics

The prototype served as both a narrative device and a functional testing ground. Players interacted with the login system, explored the wholesome hub, and triggered the glitch event, experiencing the psychological shift firsthand.

5. Testing

Testing validated that players naturally explored interactive elements and discovered hidden triggers without explicit guidance. This confirmed that the environmental storytelling approach was intuitive and effective.

6. Technical Challenges & Team Adaptability

A major challenge emerged when Twitter’s safety algorithms shadowbanned our automated character feed due to high-velocity fictional activity. This threatened to break the ARG’s cross-platform progression loop.

Our Team Pivot (Action)

To safeguard the launch window, I re-engineered the routing map, re-weighting traffic toward active Reddit and Instagram nodes. I also implemented dynamic fallback protocols and string replacement in the frontend to keep the story fluid even when users encountered restricted or broken links.

The Impact (Result)

This rapid infrastructure optimization protected the cross-media experience. Players continued to move fluidly through live external platforms, dead links were minimized, and the ARG’s illusion remained intact. The campaign launched on schedule with a stable, responsive hub at its core.

7. Reflection

Adapting the project in real time, from a planned edited final video to a live stream reveal, highlighted the importance of flexibility in collaborative media design. The live stream format allowed us to preserve the core narrative impact while enhancing the sense of real-time immersion and unpredictability.

The website, glitch effects, and livestream environment worked together to convey themes of online hate, mental health deterioration, and digital personas. This project reinforced how interactive media, when carefully engineered, can make psychological horror feel grounded rather than sensational.

Collaborating with the team, combining research on Vtubers and cyberbullying, environment design, and AI in games taught me how to integrate diverse disciplines into a cohesive interactive experience. In future projects, I plan to build on these skills in real-time storytelling, media integration, and UX-driven narrative design.
