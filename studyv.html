1. The Vision: Immersive Environmental Storytelling

For the multi-layered psychological horror ARG "Coward", my core mission was to construct an operational central hub called "VTuber Nexus" for the fictional streamer Stella, Guardian of Moths. Rather than building an isolated sandbox layout, I wanted the code to act as a porous narrative web. By tying our native UI directly to active external pipelines on Twitter, Reddit, and Instagram, the application functions as an interconnected maze that rewards analytical user tracking and blurs the lines between reality and fiction.

Cross-Functional Collaboration

To ensure our code paradigms and design assets moved smoothly across external platform environments, I worked heavily across disciplines:

  • Bridging Design & Code: Partnered closely with our 2D Lead Artist, Isabella, to optimize and slice custom assets, banner art, and UI stickers for use on both our native interface and our external social channel skins.
  • Cross-Platform Infrastructure: Managed hyperlinking frameworks and deep-linking hooks with our Writing Lead, Sam, and PR Lead, Mike, ensuring clues uncovered on Reddit and Instagram navigated users cleanly back into our secure system terminal.
  • Unified Storytelling Pipeline: Synthesized multimedia fragments, including creepy environmental render files from our 3D Specialist, Kirsty, into a responsive grid capable of handling variable cross-origin referrals.
Interactive Engineering

The Dual-State Architecture

The entire web space is engineered around progressive psychological tension, requiring technical problem-solving to dynamically transform native layouts and handle outgoing platform pathways:

🔒
The Portal Gate: A secure frontend authentication dashboard panel. Requiring specific hardcoded system credentials (admin / password123), it rewards players who discover administrative codes hidden inside live social media threads.
🔴
The Record Trigger: Hitting the 'Record' interaction interface directly displays the dynamic glitch effects on screen, operating as our narrative pivot point to instantly break down structural styles.
⚡
Dynamic Theme Degradation: Utilizing client-side JavaScript DOM overrides, dynamic merchandise pricing alterations, and corrupted image swaps to simulate a platform undergoing total data corruption after the glitch event fires.

Agile Problem Solving

Technical Challenges & Team Adaptability

The Challenge (Situation)

Our digital storytelling roadmap called for deep structural dependencies across live channels on Twitter, Reddit, and Instagram. However, right before launch, our custom automated character feed was unexpectedly flagged and shadowbanned by Twitter's safety algorithms due to its high-velocity fictional activity. This sudden break in the external chain threatened to isolate our central web hub and fracture the game's progression loop.

Our Team Pivot (Action)

Moving rapidly to safeguard our live launch window, I quickly re-engineered the website routing map. I adjusted the outbound channel directories to re-weight traffic toward our active Reddit and Instagram nodes, while simultaneously baking simulated fallbacks and dynamic string replacement protocols into the frontend code to keep the story fluid even if a user hit an algorithmically restricted dead-end.

The Impact (Result)

This rapid infrastructure optimization successfully protected our cross-media UX. It kept players moving fluidly through our live external platforms, prevented dead links from breaking immersion, and allowed our group to launch a highly successful, cross-origin alternate reality game right on schedule.
