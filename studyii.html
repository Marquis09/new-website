Working Class Heroes Website Redesign - UX Case Study

Improving the User Experience and Mobile Usability for a "SEAMLESS SHOPPING JOURNEY".

Project Details

  • My Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Duration: 4 Weeks
  • Tools: Figma, Adobe Photoshop, HTML, CSS (Bootstrap)
  • Project Type: "E-COMMERCE REDESIGN"

1. Project Summary & The Challenge

The original Working Class Heroes e-commerce website suffered from significant "USABILITY AND DESIGN FLAWS" that created friction for users trying to browse and purchase products. The main issues were a cluttered navigation, poor mobile adaptability, and inconsistencies in the checkout process.

The Challenge: To perform a targeted redesign focused on modernizing the aesthetic, simplifying the information architecture, and creating a "SEAMLESS, MOBILE-FIRST SHOPPING EXPERIENCE" to increase conversion rates and customer satisfaction.

2. Problem Statement & Design Goals

User analysis revealed that the website's technical and structural shortcomings directly impacted customer abandonment rates.

The Core Pain Points

  • Cluttered UI: Lack of clear navigation and "CONFUSING HOMEPAGE DESIGN".
  • Mobile Frustration: Poor mobile responsiveness for "ON-THE-GO USERS".
  • Checkout Friction: Inconsistent layout, particularly with product availability and "STREAMLINED CHECKOUT PROCESS".
  • Missing Essentials: Missing footer with important links like contact and social media.

Success Metrics (Goals)

  • Navigation: Improve homepage by adding clear sections and a welcoming introduction.
  • Responsiveness: Make the website "MOBILE-RESPONSIVE" for on-the-go users.
  • Usability: Incorporate a functional search bar with error prevention and "SUGGESTION PROMPTS".
  • Conversion: Revamp product pages by adding "REAL-TIME STOCK AVAILABILITY".

3. Design Process: Analysis and Ideation

The redesign followed a user-centered approach, prioritizing "INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE" and mobile adaptability.

Heuristic Review

Conducted a heuristic evaluation to identify violations of usability principles, highlighting the severe navigation and "ERROR-PREVENTION" issues.

Wireframing Focus

Designed low-fidelity wireframes, focusing on decluttering the homepage and ensuring the core shopping flow worked flawlessly on "MOBILE VIEWPORTS".

Visual Design & Prototyping

Developed high-fidelity prototypes incorporating modern e-commerce visual standards, including clearer product imagery and "STREAMLINED TYPOGRAPHY".

4. Key Design Solutions Implemented

The redesign focused on four critical areas to alleviate user pain and drive conversion:

Solution 1: Clean Homepage & Carousel

Replaced the busy, static homepage with a clean layout utilizing a "CAROUSEL" for key product categories. This provides a welcoming introduction without overwhelming the user upon arrival.

Redesigned Homepage

Solution 2: Predictive Search & Error Prevention

Introduced a clear, functional search bar with "SUGGESTIVE PROMPTS" and better "ERROR PREVENTION" to quickly guide users to products, reducing the number of 'no result' pages.

Enhanced Search Bar

Solution 3: Clear Product Availability

Revamped product pages to display "REAL-TIME STOCK AVAILABILITY" clearly *before* the user selects a size, preventing frustration and unnecessary clicks for out-of-stock items.

Product Detail Page

Solution 4: Single-Page Checkout Flow

Simplified the purchasing journey by "CONSOLIDATING ALL CHECKOUT INFORMATION" onto a single, clear page, removing redundant pop-up windows and reducing cognitive load.

Streamlined Checkout

6. Final Thoughts & Future Steps

This redesign was a valuable experience, as it allowed me to tackle real-world usability issues and improve the overall shopping experience.

  • Refinement: Continue refining the site with more dynamic features, like personalized recommendations.
