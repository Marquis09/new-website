2. Target Audience & User Needs

Target User: The Visitor

"I'm driving there now and need the GPS location quickly."

UX Solution: High-visibility "Plan Your Visit" page with a direct clickable Google Maps link to eliminate friction.

Target User: The Member

"I want to listen back to last week's teaching."

UX Solution: Direct access to a sermon archive card with clear audio-visual cues.

3. Key Design Solutions

I shifted the focus from 'hosting text' to 'guiding users' through visual storytelling and functional tools.

Solution 1: Bento Grid IA

By implementing a Bento Box logic, I reduced the "Interaction Cost." Users no longer have to dig through menus; instead, the church‚Äôs core ministries are presented as equal, scannable priorities.

Solution 2: Brand & Accessibility

I introduced a "Nature-Inspired" palette. Deep greens symbolize growth, while soft creams provide high legibility for older demographics, meeting accessibility standards (WCAG).

Solution 3: The "Plan Your Visit" Funnel

To bridge the gap between digital browsing and physical visiting, I designed a dedicated "Plan Your Visit" landing page. The centerpiece is a one-click Google Maps integration, allowing mobile users to launch GPS navigation directly from the browser.

4. Technical Reflection

This project was an exercise in Utility-First Design. The biggest improvement was cleaning up the "Wall of Text" and replacing it with actionable tools like external links and scannable grids.

Future Iterations

  • Live API: Moving from a static map link to a dynamic embedded Google Maps API.
  • RSVP Form: A simple Javascript-based contact form for new visitors.
